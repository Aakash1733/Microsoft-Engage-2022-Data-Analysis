Electric Vehicle : The demand of the future customer

India has the dubious distinction of having nine of the world's top ten most polluted cities. Greater Noida, Noida, Lucknow, and Delhi are among the nine cities in north India. While numerous things contribute to northern India's filthy air, skies, and human lungs, automobile pollution plays a significant role. As a result, it's predictable that the Indian government is gradually but steadily supporting the use of electric vehicles. And, in terms of our ability to commute, we may have come full circle. While electric vehicles (EVs) lost out against gasoline-powered vehicles in the 1900s, this may no longer be the case. An EV is a vehicle that runs on electricity rather than gasoline and has a battery instead of a gas tank. EVs have reduced operating costs and are also environmentally benign because they have fewer moving parts. In India, the cost of an electric vehicle's fuel is around 80 paisa per kilometre. Compare this to the cost of petrol in Indian cities, which is now over Rs 100 per litre, or Rs 7-8 per kilometre to operate a petrol-powered vehicle. The private sector has recognised that the EV's domination is unavoidable. EVs are being used for deliveries by companies such as Amazon, Swiggy, Zomato, and Ikea. Automobile manufacturers like as Mahindra are cooperating with consumers such as Ola, while Tata Motors is partnering with Blu Smart Mobility to assure more electric vehicle delivery and ride-hailing services.

But , this fact must not be neglected that the availability of credit may not surpass self buy, it still doubles the sale of vehicle . As customers dependent on credit wont come in the class of potential buyers if credits are over .

Detailed Analysis

As a manufacturer, one of the most difficult decisions you'll have to make is whether to use petrol or diesel in your vehicle. Diesel automobiles, on the one hand, have a low fuel cost and great fuel economy, yet petrol cars have a higher RPM and beginning acceleration, making them more powerful. For a handful of the major brands, we've shown a comparison of the number of sold quantities of petrol and diesel variants. Maruti Suzuki is the market leader in both types, with approximately 126 thousand petrol variants and 137 thousand diesel variants sold. Also, we can see that, with a 6:4 ratio, petrol variants continue to outnumber diesel variants in total.
